Policy

Ownership & Use

This page explains what belongs to NPDS, what remains the property of schools or third parties, and how platform materials should be treated.

Brand and Source Ownership

National Prep Deaf Sports preserves records from many sources, but the platform itself has its own identity, presentation, and boundaries around reuse.

NPDS Platform Materials

The National Prep Deaf Sports name, NPDS shorthand, branding, original written content, site presentation, and overall platform structure are part of NPDS and should not be copied or republished as if they were an unrelated commercial product.

School and Third-Party Marks

School names, logos, mascots, and other third-party marks remain the property of their respective schools, organizations, or rights holders.

Commercial Reuse

The compiled presentation, structure, and branding of NPDS should not be reused commercially without permission, even when underlying sports facts may originate from public or school-provided sources.

Respect for Sources

NPDS may reference school-provided records, archived publications, and other historical material, but source context and ownership should still be respected when content is quoted, redistributed, or republished.

Brand Integrity

Use of the NPDS name should remain consistent with the platform's actual mission and should not imply endorsements, partnerships, or official authority that do not exist.
